## Critical Context Discovered

Before recommendations: two facts surfaced during research that shape everything.

**1. Kingshot players are called "Governors."** This is the official in-game player title per Century Games' own description: *"Players assume the role of Governors."* Any label or advisor name should feel congruent with this. The advisor serves a Governor.

**2. No named advisor characters exist in competing mobile strategy games.** Rise of Kingdoms, Lords Mobile, Whiteout Survival, Clash of Clans — none have a memorable named advisor character. This is not a crowded space. This is a first-mover opportunity. The character can become iconic because nothing else in the genre has nailed it.

## Task 1: AI Advisor Character Name

### The Vizier — REJECT

**Verdict: Do not use.**

The association is not theoretical. It is confirmed across multiple independent sources:

- **Disney's Jafar** (*Aladdin*, 1992) is the dominant Western pop culture "Vizier" — a gaunt, scheming sorcerer who betrays the ruler he serves. The character is culturally ubiquitous across every confirmed market (US, Brazil, Germany).
- **Prince of Persia** (1989, original game and sequels) features Jafar as the scheming vizier attempting to usurp the throne by deception.
- These two Jafar incarnations have fused "Vizier" with *treachery* in Western pop culture — the opposite of what a trusted personal advisor needs.

The cultural origin (Arabic/Ottoman chief minister) is historically neutral-to-positive and resonant for Turkish and Indonesian audiences. The problem is not the word's origin. The problem is that the pop culture override is nearly total for the Western half of your audience.

### Top 3 Recommended Names

---

**#1 — THE STEWARD**
*Recommended primary choice*

**What it means:** In medieval tradition, the Steward manages the ruler's household and realm in their absence. In *Lord of the Rings*, the Stewards of Gondor are the loyal guardians who keep the kingdom running when the king cannot. In *Crusader Kings*, Stewards handle domain affairs, estates, and resources — exactly what this advisor does.

**Gaming associations:** Tolkien resonance (Stewards of Gondor, loyal and devoted). Crusader Kings stewards as trusted domain managers. No villain associations confirmed in research.

**Why it fits Kingshot:** A Governor's Steward manages the realm while the Governor fights. The metaphor is precise. It implies devoted personal service — "knows your account" is exactly what a Steward does.

**Global fit:**
- US/Brazil/Germany: Tolkien-literate audience reads this positively
- Indonesia/Vietnam: Universally understood as loyal caretaker (parallels local cultural roles)
- Turkey: Links to Ottoman household management roles — honest service connotation

**Trustworthiness:** High. Servile, loyal, personal. Not a power-grabbing title.

**Risk:** Could feel administrative rather than strategic. Denethor (LOTR Steward) is flawed and arguably villainous — but this is a minor flag, not a dominant association.

---

**#2 — THE SAGE**
*Strongest alternative*

**What it means:** Universal wisdom archetype. Ancient, knowledgeable, above politics.

**Gaming associations:** Final Fantasy sages (magic-users, teachers). Civilization tech advisors. Village elders in Witcher and Skyrim. Broadly positive across every genre.

**Why it fits Kingshot:** The advisor "knows" your account — Sage implies deep knowledge. For Phase 2 (real AI), "Your Sage has analyzed your forces" carries well.

**Global fit:** Excellent across all six markets. "Sage" or equivalent wisdom archetypes exist in every culture — guru (Indonesia/Vietnam), hakim (Turkey/Arabic contexts), weiser Mann (Germany). No translation friction.

**Trustworthiness:** High — wise, patient, non-threatening. Slightly more detached than Steward (oracle vs. loyal servant).

**Risk:** Feels slightly impersonal. More "distant wisdom" than "knows you personally." The Phase 1 scripted version may feel hollow under a Sage name that implies deep intelligence.

---

**#3 — THE HERALD**
*Distinctive option worth considering*

**What it means:** The Herald knows the ruler's full title, standing, and history — and announces it to the world. In medieval tradition, Heralds were the official record-keepers of lineage, rank, and battle achievements.

**Why it's interesting:** No other gaming dashboard uses this title. It maps specifically to what this character does: reads your FID, knows your account history, and tells you what you are. "Your Herald has your record" feels natural.

**Gaming associations:** Minor — some RPG quest-givers use Herald, but no dominant villain associations. Clean slate.

**Global fit:** Moderate. Less immediately familiar than Steward or Sage in some markets, but no negative associations anywhere.

**Trustworthiness:** High. The Herald speaks truth by oath.

**Risk:** Less warm and loyal-feeling than Steward. More official/announcer. May feel formal rather than personal.

---

### Cultural/Sensitivity Flags — Advisor Name

- **Archon** (was considered): Research flagged villain associations in Mass Effect (Archon = enemy boss) and Warhammer. Feels commanding/distant rather than trusted advisor. Not recommended.
- **The Keeper**: Research flagged hostile entity associations in Minecraft, Warframe, Dark Souls. Not recommended.
- **Hand of the King**: Immediately recognizable from Game of Thrones but carries HBO IP associations and has a murder rate problem (they keep dying).
- **Seneschal**: Too obscure for global audience.
- **Chancellor**: Scheming associations in some Warcraft lore. Weak.

---

### Herd Lens Flag — Advisor Name

⚠️ The ranking of Steward > Sage > Herald emerged primarily from one Perplexity synthesis query. It is internally reasoned but not multi-source confirmed. The Vizier rejection is firmly multi-sourced. The positive recommendation should be validated with a small community test (poll in r/Kingshot or the KingshotPro Discord when it exists) before committing.

## Task 2: Spending Tier Labels

### The Marine Animal Framework — Use With Caution

**The whale/dolphin/minnow/shrimp framework is real, established, and widely known in gaming communities.** It dates to 2011 (GamesBrief) and is used across gacha gaming Reddit communities. Average mobile strategy players have encountered these terms.

**However:**
- Dolphin is **not used with pride** by mid-tier spenders. It's a neutral middle tier, not a badge.
- Whale is **self-claimed with status energy** by top spenders — it works as a top-tier label.
- Minnow and Shrimp have no pride attachment. They're developer vocabulary, not player identity.
- These are **developer/analyst terms** — they classify from the outside in. The goal here is labels that feel like *player identity from the inside out*.

**The real issue with showing labels directly to players:** Research confirms that label type strongly influences user perception. Labels feel empowering when they map to how you *play* (agency), not how much you *paid* (transaction). The marine animal framework maps to wallet, not playstyle.

---

### Recommended System: Playstyle Archetype Labels

The greeting: *"Welcome back, [name]. You're a [label] Governor on a [server age] server."*

Note: "Governor" is the in-game player title. Including it in the greeting reinforces game identity and makes the label feel like a modifier to their earned title, not a spending category.

| Internal Tier | Label | Logic |
|---|---|---|
| F2P (pay_amt = 0) | **Free Commander** | Keeps the "F2P" badge of pride — F2P is already embraced in communities. "Commander" adds rank/dignity without changing the identity. Players who are F2P say it with pride — this respects that. |
| Low Spender ($1–$99) | **Tactician** | Implies strategic, targeted spending — maximizing value from selective investments. Agency frame: you chose where to spend. No judgment implied. |
| Mid Spender ($100–$499) | **Veteran** | Earned through sustained commitment. Implies experience and reliability, not just money spent. Universally respected label in gaming communities. |
| Whale ($500+) | **Warlord** | Status energy that high spenders will wear with pride. Power frame, not just money frame. Research confirms whale players often self-identify with dominance language. |

---

### How to Read the Greeting

> "Welcome back, Ravensword. You're a **Warlord** Governor on a 180-day server."

> "Welcome back, Nyx. You're a **Veteran** Governor on a 95-day server."

> "Welcome back, Ironside. You're a **Free Commander** Governor on a 310-day server."

The label modifies "Governor" — which is already their earned, in-game title. Nobody is reduced to a purchase. They are a type of Governor.

---

### Why Not the Alternatives

**Option B (medieval feudal titles: Freeman/Knight/Lord/King):**
- "Freeman" reads as someone who doesn't pay — the not-paying-for-anything meaning dominates over the historical meaning
- The hierarchy is fully transparent (everyone knows Knight < Lord < King) — this makes the spending judgment obvious and unavoidable
- "Peasant" for F2P would be catastrophic. Even "Freeman" is problematic. Rejected.

**Option C (game stage: Early Kingdom/Rising Kingdom/etc.):**
- Brilliant in theory — spend-neutral, maps to progression
- But this loses the targeting precision that makes KingshotPro valuable. The advisor needs to know spend tier to give relevant advice. The label primes the player for the advisory tone that follows.
- Could be used as a secondary descriptor but not as the primary label.

**Marine animals adapted for medieval theme (hybrid):**
- Feels gimmicky. No clean medieval equivalent for Dolphin.
- Loses the community recognition of the original framework without gaining the dignity of a purpose-built system.

---

### Cultural/Sensitivity Flags — Tier Labels

- **"Warlord"** for top tier: In English gaming culture, this reads as powerful and earned. However, in Indonesian, Vietnamese, and Chinese historical contexts, "warlord" (軍閥/warlord era) carries civilian-suffering associations. The English word in an English-language dashboard used by global players is likely fine — but be aware this label may land differently in those markets than intended. Monitor community response post-launch.
- **"Freeman"**: Do not use. Tested poorly in research framing. The "free from paying" meaning overrides the historical dignity of the word.
- **"Peasant"**: Obviously do not use for any tier.

---

### Herd Lens Flag — Tier Labels

⚠️ The recommendation against dolphin/minnow being used with pride is supported by multiple Reddit threads. The recommendation FOR playstyle archetypes is supported by UX psychology research. However, no A/B testing data exists for these specific labels in a gaming dashboard context. The "Warlord" recommendation for top tier is confident — high spenders in strategy games consistently respond to dominance language. The "Tactician" label for low-tier is reasoned but untested as a pride label. Worth A/B testing at launch.
