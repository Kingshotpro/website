# Oath and Bone — STORY.md

*Scene-by-scene script. Expands DESIGN.md §4 into the 12-battle three-act spine, four branch nodes, three endings, bond triggers, Act 3 antagonist, and the voice-barb style guide that scales to 500+ combat lines without drift.*

*Written 2026-04-20. Foundation: DESIGN.md, MAGIC.md, HEROES.md, FRAME_UPDATE.md, SUCCESSION_V2.md. Prior session's STORY_SUCCESSION.md tether-seed considered, tested against MAGIC.md, partially adopted, extended.*

---

## 0. How this document is used

STORY.md is the **writing bible**. Every delegation prompt for dialogue, every cutscene board, every battle-text generator must cite it. It is the canon against which generated lines are graded.

Three rules for anyone — Claude or Architect — drafting new prose from this file:

1. **The voice is terse, weighted, non-theatrical.** No speeches. No exposition. Lines do work; they do not announce. If a line could be cut, it is cut. See §8.
2. **Permadeath is real in the prose.** Never write a line that cannot honor losing that speaker. Branch and bond text must read cleanly whether the hero lives or a stand-in carries the line.
3. **The antagonist is not a villain. She is a mistake that kept walking.** Do not give her monologues. Give her silence, residue, and one or two lines that cost to speak. See §5.

**What this file does not do:** balance, scenario mechanics, map layout. Those live in `BATTLES.md` (stub). This file gives BATTLES.md the *feel* each scenario must deliver. BATTLES.md decides hex counts.

## 1. OPENING CUTSCENE — *"The Ring That Does Not Fit"*

**Length target:** ~90 seconds of screen time. Hand-painted stills with camera push/pull, no animation beyond ring-rotation and page-turn. Audio: one sustained note, a cello, low register, no melody. Wind. Crows once.

### Frame 1 — EXTREME CLOSE-UP

A signet ring on a gloved hand. Silver, the finish dulled from wear. A tower-and-crown sigil is pressed into black wax on a letter lying beneath. The crown is half-smudged — someone's thumb has been on it recently.

The ring *slides*. Down one joint. Back up. Down again. The hand is not moving; the ring is. It is loose.

**Caption (italicized lower-third, stays on screen 2 seconds):** *Ashreach Keep. Eight weeks after the funeral of Lord Torren Thorne.*

### Frame 2 — PULL OUT, MEDIUM SHOT

A 26-year-old woman in mail-and-leather half-armor stands at a narrow window. Winter light, thin, eastern. She is holding an open ledger on the stone sill. Her breath is visible. The room is cold; someone has not lit the fire for her yet.

She is **Vael Thorne**. She does not know where she stands in the room yet. Her posture is her brother's — adopted, not lived. The armor was fitted to him first.

### Frame 3 — CLOSE ON LEDGER

A page is torn out. The stub at the spine has writing on it — not ink. Red-brown, scraped. Small marks. Like a letter scratched with a fingernail dipped in something the body makes.

(The player will not be told, yet, what it is. Returning players will recognize it in Battle 8.)

### Frame 4 — PAGE TURN MONTAGE (8 beats, 12 seconds total)

Vael turns a page. Torn. Turn. Torn. Turn. Torn. Seven pages missing, eight weeks accounted for, each stub carrying the same red-brown residue at the tear line. On the eighth page, her brother's handwriting resumes — ordinary, kingdom-ledger — as if nothing had happened.

She stops. Stares at the torn edge. Touches it. Her thumb comes away faintly dusted.

### Frame 5 — CLOSE ON VAEL'S FACE

She is not crying. She has not yet decided to feel. A younger sister holding the evidence of a brother she did not know enough. She is deciding what to do with it.

She closes the ledger. Sets it on the sill. Opens her hand and looks at the ring.

It spins on her finger, once, freely. She closes her fist around it to keep it in place.

### Frame 6 — EXTERIOR, WIDE

Ashreach Keep. A hill fortress in a borderland of gray stone and gray pines. The banners of House Thorne — a lone tower against a barred field — hang at half-mast. It has been eight weeks; someone forgot, or chose, not to raise them.

A column of riders crests the valley road. Six horses. Road-tired. The lead rider dismounts in the courtyard. He is **Sergeant Halv** — 44, grey at the temples, rain-damp, one fist pressed flat to his chestplate in the old salute.

He looks up at the keep's eastern window. She is still there.

### Frame 7 — COURTYARD

Vael descends the stair. Her boots land too loud on the stone. She has not learned to walk lightly yet.

**HALV** *(quiet, careful):* My lord. The hollow's dead are dead again. Not from the crossing.

She crosses to him. Her right hand closes on the left — instinct, keeping the ring on.

**VAEL:** Show me.

**HALV:** There's smoke off the ridge. Kavess rides in from the south. And —

*(he hesitates, which is uncharacteristic, which is the point)*

**HALV:** Brin's awake. She wants to talk to you before we ride.

A long look between them. He knows why Brin wants to talk. So do we, by the end of the chapter.

### Frame 8 — HOLD

On Vael's hand. The ring catches the light. It is loose. It is a man's ring. It belongs to a dead man whose pages are missing.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

### TITLE CARD

Hand-painted, gold-leaf on black. One word at a time, 0.5-second beats between.

> **OATH.**
> **AND BONE.**
>
> *Chapter 1 — The Borderland Seat*

---

### Visual WOW notes for this opening

- The ring is shot like a murder weapon. We will see it again in Acts 2 and 3, and it will have changed twice.
- The ledger page with red-brown residue is the first appearance of **Vellum-script** (§5). Every returning viewer who reaches Battle 8 re-watches this opening with the key in hand and sees what they missed.
- **Vael never says her brother's name in Act 1.** First time she says "Torren" aloud is Battle 8's post-battle beat. The player will feel the weight accumulating without knowing why.
- The ring's looseness is a visible timer. Artists must be instructed: the ring gets *progressively less loose* across Acts 1–2, and in Act 3 fits perfectly — a horror moment, because it means the tether is taking.

## 2. ACT 1 — THE BORDERLAND SEAT (Battles 1–4)

**Arc:** Vael inherits. Discovers the world has a second layer. Is forced to decide what kind of lord she will be before she knows what she is inheriting.

**Tonal register:** mundane turning strange. The magic is off-screen; its consequences are on-screen.

---

### BATTLE 1 — *"The Muster"*

**Location:** the East Crossing, two hours' ride below Ashreach. Plain biome. One ridge feature, low. Morning.

**Tutorial freight:** troop triangle, elevation, move/attack/hold.

**Party at start:** Vael + Halv + Brin (3 heroes, locked composition).

#### Pre-battle (6 lines)

> **[Wide: a thin column of smoke climbing off a burned wagon. Four figures standing in the grass around it. One is kneeling. The kneeler is Brin.]**
>
> **HALV:** Same crossing. Different wagon.
> **VAEL:** How many of ours?
> **HALV:** Two carters. A boy. And a cavalry scout who shouldn't have been this far out.
> **BRIN** *(rising, not turning):* My lord. I told him to hold the north track. He rode south.
> **VAEL:** *(quiet)* We'll talk after.
> **HALV:** *(scanning the ridge)* They're still here.

*[Enemy banner appears over ridge: kingdom colors, not theirs. A rival vassal's infantry squad, opportunistic on a weakened seat.]*

#### Post-battle — VICTORY (7 lines)

> **HALV:** Clean work, my lord.
> **VAEL:** Who were they?
> **HALV:** Karrowmen. Lord Orik's pennant, second company. They'll say it was mistaken identity.
> **BRIN:** It wasn't.
> **VAEL:** No. It wasn't.
> **HALV:** *(after a pause)* Your brother used to say Orik was the kind of man who watched funerals to count the swords that didn't come.
> **VAEL:** *(looking at the ridge, not at Halv)* Then he's been counting for eight weeks.

*[Rewards. Advisor grants 60 XP, 1 credit. Observation: `hero_composition` = martial.]*

#### Post-battle — DEFEAT (4 lines)

> **HALV:** Pull back. Pull back to the keep.
> **BRIN:** The boy —
> **HALV:** *(hard)* We'll come back for them.
> *[Retry or return to camp. No XP; advisor line: "Every commander loses one. Twice, and it's a pattern."]*

### BATTLE 2 — *"The Hollow"*

**Location:** Eastmarch Hollow, old-growth forest. Rough + forest biome. One river crossing. Midday becoming overcast.

**Teaches:** terrain (forest cover), line of sight, grove-biome introduction.

**Scout-in:** a druid sigil is found carved into a trunk. Brin recognizes it. First on-screen brush with Druidry without explanation.

#### Pre-battle (8 lines)

> **[A clearing. Four dead deer, arranged in a ring, heads pointing inward. The grass under them is wrong — pale, curled, as if something pulled the life from beneath.]**
>
> **BRIN:** I grew up in these woods. I have never seen that.
> **HALV:** Poachers?
> **BRIN:** Poachers want the meat.
> **HALV:** Wolves?
> **BRIN:** Wolves don't arrange.
> **VAEL:** What arranges deer like this?
> **BRIN:** *(looking at the tree-line)* Somebody who wanted somebody else to see it.
> **VAEL:** Then we give them what they want. Formation.

*[Enemy reveal: bandits on paper. Closer look: they carry bone-hilt knives. Necromancer-reagent harvesters — though the player is not told yet.]*

#### Post-battle — VICTORY (6 lines)

> **[One enemy is taken alive. Brin kneels to search him. Finds a pouch of chalk-dust and grave-salt. Halv tenses.]**
>
> **BRIN:** *(holding up a pinch)* This isn't dye.
> **HALV:** *(to Vael, flat)* If you want my counsel — burn his kit and bury him shallow. Leave him for the hollow.
> **VAEL:** Is that what my brother would do.
> **HALV:** *(beat)* Your brother didn't know what this was.
> **VAEL:** Then neither do I.
>
> *[Choice prompt: **Burn the kit** (Halv +1, grim register) / **Keep the kit** (investigation flag +1, Caelen recruitment weighted earlier).]*

#### Post-battle — DEFEAT (3 lines)

> **BRIN:** *(carrying a wounded Halv out of the trees)* I've lost people in these woods. I don't lose people in these woods.
> **HALV:** Get her home.
> **BRIN:** *(not answering)*

### BATTLE 3 — *"The Crypt Gate"*

**Location:** Fort Raven, a century-sealed ruin below the valley. Ruin biome. Elevation heavy — three tiers, the crypt mouth at the lowest point. Dusk.

**Teaches:** fighting downslope; first encounter with a summoned creature (a bone-wraith, not a troop). The world opens.

**Druid gate:** if player kept the kit in B2 and the party has Brin alive, **Thessa of the Hollow** appears mid-battle as a tremor-aid — she steps from a seam in a standing stone, heals one ally, speaks one line, and is gone. She is recruitable after B3 in this branch.

#### Pre-battle (9 lines)

> **[The party stands above the crypt gate. The stone door has been opened from the inside. The hinges are rusted into a half-closed position but the door moved. Recently.]**
>
> **HALV:** This fort was sealed by the second Thorne. My father's father.
> **VAEL:** Why.
> **HALV:** Nobody ever told me.
> **BRIN:** *(kneeling by the door-seam)* Somebody's been here this week. Boot's Karrowman.
> **VAEL:** Orik, again.
> **HALV:** He shouldn't know this place exists.
> **BRIN:** He doesn't. Somebody sent him.
> **HALV:** Who sends a rival vassal to open a sealed crypt?
> **VAEL:** *(without looking back)* We're about to find out.

#### Mid-battle scripted beat

At turn 4, if HP of any party member drops below 40%, a bone-wraith rises from the crypt mouth — **first non-troop enemy in the game**. The screen darkens 15%. A choral dissonance stinger plays. Any veteran caster present reacts:

> **CAELEN** *(if present — not in B3 by default; reserved for later battles)*
> **BRIN** *(if present, default):* What *is* —
> **HALV:** *(under breath)* Oh, my lord.

If Thessa-gate triggered (see above), at turn 6 a second wraith appears, and Thessa steps between the party and it:

> **THESSA:** *(not looking at Vael, addressing the wraith)* Not here.
> *[She casts Root. The wraith freezes in place. She turns to Vael.]*
> **THESSA:** You weren't ready for this. Neither is the wraith. Finish it quickly.

#### Post-battle — VICTORY (8 lines)

> **[The party stands at the crypt mouth. The door has not been closed. Inside, far down, a faint cold wind. Something is still breathing down there.]**
>
> **VAEL:** We close this.
> **HALV:** We can't close what's already open, my lord.
> **BRIN:** *(quietly)* We can seal it again.
> **HALV:** Not without a mason who knows the old cut. And a priest. And —
> **THESSA** *(if present; otherwise line given to Brin):* And someone who knows what opened it.
> **VAEL:** Then find me one.
> **HALV:** *(reluctant)* There's a man in the Ice-Lakes hold. Imprisoned. He'll know.
> **VAEL:** The name.
> **HALV:** Marrow.

*[Rewards + Act 1 flag: `crypt_opened = true`. Advisor observation: `magic_school_contact` = 'necromancy'.]*

#### Post-battle — DEFEAT (3 lines)

> **HALV:** *(dragging Vael clear)* We leave the gate. We leave the gate, my lord.
> **VAEL:** *(over her shoulder, as the wraith returns to the dark)* I saw it.
> **HALV:** So did it.

### BATTLE 4 — *"The Ridge"* 🔀 **BRANCH NODE**

**Location:** Karrow Ridge, the boundary between Ashreach and Lord Orik's seat. Ridge biome. High elevation both sides; the ridge is a spine. Noon. A parley tent pitched at the summit. Banners present but no blades drawn — yet.

**Teaches:** diplomacy → combat transitions; first branch node.

**Branch trigger:** Lord Orik the Ashen presents Vael with three options. The choice determines whether Orik becomes an Act 3 ally (§2.4 recruitment spec in HEROES.md: Orik is an Act 3 recruit "if branching allows").

#### Pre-battle (10 lines)

> **[The parley tent. Orik is 50, a black-iron cavalry commander with a scar crossing his upper lip. His second stands behind him — silent. A map of the borderland is on the table.]**
>
> **ORIK:** You've held the seat eight weeks.
> **VAEL:** I've held the seat eight weeks.
> **ORIK:** Your brother held it twelve years before me and mine held the ridge. He never swore to the crown directly. He swore to the cavalry banner. Mine.
> **VAEL:** My brother did what my brother did.
> **ORIK:** Yes. And now he doesn't. So we're here.
> *(he turns the map toward her)*
> **ORIK:** My terms. You swear to me the way he did. I hold the ridge. You hold the seat. The crown sees one banner, not two, and we both survive the next winter.
> **HALV:** *(behind Vael)* My lord.
> **ORIK:** *(not looking at Halv)* Old man. Your counsel is noted.
> **VAEL:** What if I don't swear.
> **ORIK:** Then we find out which of us loses the ridge first.

#### Branch options (player-facing UI: three choices, all unlock battle)

**OPTION A — Submit** *(Orik becomes patron; easier Act 2 politics; Halv goes flat; Orik unrecruitable later)*
> **VAEL:** I swear to the ridge-banner. Under the old forms.
> **ORIK:** Good lord.
> **HALV:** *(quiet, behind)* Gods forgive you.
> *[Battle: skirmish against Karrow raiders who didn't get the message. Orik fights alongside. Tutorial on cavalry mechanics.]*

**OPTION B — Refuse** *(hostility with Orik; harder Act 2; "Endures" ending remains reachable)*
> **VAEL:** The seat swears to the crown. Not to you.
> **ORIK:** *(after a long beat)* Then the seat learns.
> *[Battle: Orik's banner vs Vael's, full 6v6. Permadeath alert. Halv gains +2 morale; Brin gains a permanent voice-barb: "He was always like that."]*

**OPTION C — Ally-as-Equal** *(requires Brin present and B2 Keep-the-Kit choice; unlocks "Widens" ending; Orik recruitable at B11)*
> **VAEL:** No oath either way. But I tell you what Halv and I saw at the crypt gate yesterday.
> **ORIK:** *(skeptical)* Crypt gates are for priests.
> **VAEL:** Not this one.
> *(she unrolls a sketch Brin made — the arranged deer, the bone-salt pouch)*
> **ORIK:** *(studying it for a long time)*
> **ORIK:** …who sent us to that crypt.
> **VAEL:** That's the question.
> **ORIK:** *(finally, quiet)* Then I'm not sworn to you and you're not sworn to me. But the ridge stands between us and whatever's doing this. And I don't stand alone on a ridge.
> *[Battle: joint defense against third-party raid — the same harvesters from B2, in force. Orik fights with the party. He remembers.]*

#### Post-battle (lines vary by branch; all end with):

> **HALV:** *(as the column rides home)* That was the first decision you made without him.
> **VAEL:** Without who.
> **HALV:** *(long look)* …without me, my lord.

*[Observation: `moral_choice` = 'orik_submit' / 'orik_refuse' / 'orik_equal'. Flag: `act1_branch_closed = true`.]*

## 3. ACT 2 — THE HIDDEN WORLD (Battles 5–8)

**Arc:** Vael commits to learning what her brother died inside of. The magic becomes playable. The world gets bigger and she gets smaller in it. By B8, she has the name of the thing that killed Torren.

**Tonal register:** wonder crossed with dread. The game lets the player cast their first spell in B5 and feel, for 30 seconds, like this is a power fantasy. Then B6.

---

### BATTLE 5 — *"The Tower"*

**Location:** The Wizard's Tower, west of the seat. A seven-story stone shaft in a forgotten valley, bases overgrown. Ruin + sanctum biome. Night. The tower's lamps are lit for the first time in twenty years.

**Teaches:** Wizardry mechanics (Mana regen on sanctum tiles), Caelen's spell roster.

**Beat:** the tower is being *attacked* when the party arrives. Not by Orik. By Vellum-marked mercenaries — the first time the player sees her sigil on living soldiers. The circle-and-line.

#### Pre-battle (9 lines)

> **[Night. The valley is full of moving torches. At the tower's base, a gaunt man in once-fine robes stands in the doorway. He is **Caelen**. He does not appear tired. He appears, somehow, to have been waiting a long time.]**
>
> **CAELEN:** You took longer than I guessed.
> **VAEL:** You expected me?
> **CAELEN:** I expected *someone* with your brother's seal. I hoped it wouldn't be a child.
> **VAEL:** I'm twenty-six.
> **CAELEN:** I know.
> *(he glances past her at the torches closing in)*
> **CAELEN:** We have about four minutes before the tower becomes a tomb. I can hold the second floor, not the first. If you will stand on the first with me for those four minutes, I will come with you afterward and tell you why your brother was killed.
> **VAEL:** *(no hesitation)* Where do you want me.
> **CAELEN:** *(almost smiling)* Well said.

#### Mid-battle tutorial

Caelen opens the battle by casting Firebolt on the lead attacker. Tutorial callout: **Cast — spend Mana for spells.** The player's first use of the Cast verb is on Turn 2. Channel interrupt mechanic introduced — if Caelen is hit mid-cast, damage lands AND spell aborts. Player learns to shield him.

Enemy composition: 6 mercenaries + 1 necromantic lieutenant (the first Necro-archetype AI encounter). On the lieutenant's death, the screen holds. The corpse's left sleeve has fallen back. **Triple-loop** tattoo on the inner forearm — three interlocking rings — same as Marrow's. The player does not yet know what it means, but the shape is now in their eye.

#### Post-battle — VICTORY (9 lines)

> **[The tower, dawn. Caelen is at a long table, pouring tea. Vael is looking at the dead lieutenant's neck through the open doorway.]**
>
> **VAEL:** What is that mark.
> **CAELEN:** A student's mark.
> **VAEL:** Student of what.
> **CAELEN:** *(after a beat)* Of the woman who killed your brother.
> **VAEL:** *(quiet)* Name.
> **CAELEN:** She goes by **Esra Vellum**. It is not the name she was born with. She took it when she began doing what she does. It is the material she writes on.
> **HALV:** *(from the doorway)* Writes what.
> **CAELEN:** Bindings. On skin. The living kind.
> **VAEL:** And my brother.
> **CAELEN:** *(meeting her eyes)* I believe your brother was one of her pages.

*[Caelen joins. Wizardry unlocked. Advisor observation: `magic_school_affinity` = 'wizardry' OR 'balanced' depending on composition. Flag: `vellum_named = true`.]*

#### Post-battle — DEFEAT

*(Caelen carries the wounded party down into the tower's cellar. Tower lamps go out one by one. Soft cut; retry screen offers "fall back and rest" — replay with Caelen's aid pre-joined.)*

### BATTLE 6 — *"The Pact"* 🔀 **BRANCH NODE**

**Location:** Ice-Lakes Keep, a rival kingdom's fortress. The party rides under a flag of parley to request a prisoner. Ruin + water biome. Pre-dawn. The execution is at sunrise.

**Teaches:** Necromancy mechanics (Souls gained on kill); moral-weight branching.

**The setup:** Marrow is in a cell in the sub-keep. He will be hanged at sunrise. The Ice-Lakes warden has refused the parley; the party must fight their way to him. Marrow has been tortured — not broken. He expects to die.

Caelen does not want to be here. Thessa, if recruited, is physically ill entering the prison.

#### Pre-battle (10 lines)

> **[A cell. Torchlight. Marrow is 31, severe, not theatrically sinister — the face of a man who decided something unforgivable and lives with it on purpose. One arm broken. He is composing himself to die.]**
>
> **MARROW:** You're Thorne's sister.
> **VAEL:** Yes.
> **MARROW:** He's dead.
> **VAEL:** Yes.
> **MARROW:** Then you want the same thing I want and we have about thirty minutes before the sun makes the point moot.
> **VAEL:** What do you want.
> **MARROW:** Esra Vellum, face-down in a grave I dig.
> **VAEL:** And what do I get.
> **MARROW:** A necromancer who hates her more than you do.
> *(he lifts his head; light catches the base of his neck; the same circle-and-line)*
> **MARROW:** …and somebody who can teach you to read her writing before she finishes writing you.

#### Branch options (three — the last is a trap)

**OPTION A — Leave him** *(Marrow executes offscreen; Wizard Path unlocks; harder Act 3; "Endures" still reachable)*
> **VAEL:** *(not looking at him)* Warden. Proceed as ordered.
> **MARROW:** *(unsurprised)* …good lord.
> *[No battle. Vael walks out. Sunrise. A single off-screen drop. Halv's voice-barb for the next two battles: *"Cleaner that way."* Caelen is silent for the next full scene.]*

**OPTION B — Free and Trust** *(fight to extract; Marrow joins unbound; required for "Endures" and "Widens")*
> **VAEL:** *(to the warden, not to Marrow)* By the compact of the borderland parley, I claim him.
> **WARDEN:** Denied.
> **VAEL:** Then we go through you.
> *[Battle: 4 party heroes vs keep garrison + 2 Necro-aware AI. Marrow fights alongside, **unequipped** — no gear, one hand. If Marrow falls during extraction, scenario auto-fails.]*

**OPTION C — Free and Leash** *(a binding glyph is laid on Marrow in his cell by a complicit court-scribe; Marrow is compelled to obey; unlocks "Falls" ending; Thessa's loyalty drops permanently)*
> **VAEL:** I take him. Under glyph.
> **MARROW:** *(first reaction — surprise, then something colder)* …you already know the writing.
> **VAEL:** No. But Caelen does enough to draw it. And it comes off if you earn it off.
> **MARROW:** *(quiet)* Then I am your second page.
> *[Battle unfolds; Marrow fights compelled, not willing. After, camp dialogue is different — Thessa refuses to cross any room he's in.]*

#### Post-battle (varies per branch). Universal closer:

> **MARROW** *(if freed and trusting):* I'll tell you what her marks mean before we sleep. Not tonight. Tomorrow.
> **MARROW** *(if leashed):* You'll get your teaching. I don't have a choice.
> **MARROW** *(if left to die — spoken by Caelen, quietly):* He would have taught us, you know.

*[Observation: `moral_choice` = 'marrow_leave' / 'marrow_trust' / 'marrow_leash'. Flag: `marrow_state`.]*

### BATTLE 7 — *"The Grove Under Siege"* 🔀 **BRANCH NODE**

**Location:** The Druid's Grove, an old-growth hollow. Forest + grove biome. Twilight. A kingdom host has reached its outer ring to "cleanse the heretics." Their commander is a crown-captain acting on Esra Vellum's fed intelligence — though he does not know her name.

**Teaches:** Druidry mechanics (Verdance gain near natural tiles); terrain manipulation; the moral cost of choosing.

**Beat:** Thessa is in the center of the grove performing a rite when the party arrives. She does not stop the rite for Vael. The player chooses whether to defend or abandon while Thessa keeps her head down over the ritual fire.

#### Pre-battle (8 lines)

> **[The outer ring. Crown banners on every hill. A captain on horseback is reading an execution order to nobody in particular. Behind him, the host is waiting for the signal.]**
>
> **VAEL:** The grove is under my borderland.
> **CAPTAIN:** By the crown's seal, it is under nobody's borderland today.
> **VAEL:** On whose word.
> **CAPTAIN:** *(producing a scroll)* Signed by the High Seneschal. Sealed in court.
> *(Caelen reads the bottom corner of the seal and goes very still)*
> **CAELEN** *(to Vael, low):* That hand drew the seal. But the glyph under the hand is **hers**.
> **VAEL:** *(to the captain)* Stand your host down. That order is not clean.
> **CAPTAIN:** Lord Thorne, I have my orders.

#### Branch options (two)

**OPTION A — Defend** *(Thessa's loyalty locks at max; "Widens" pathway opens; Act 3 Hollow Child recruit available if Thessa lives)*
> **VAEL:** Then your orders break on my spears. Form up.
> *[Battle: party + grove-druids (NPC allies) vs crown host. Major engagement. Thorn Grove and Living Terrain tutorial. If Thessa dies here, campaign continues but loses an entire arm.]*

**OPTION B — Abandon** *(Thessa leaves the party at end of battle; "Falls" pathway opens; Crown relationship preserved for B10 Coerce option)*
> **VAEL:** *(long beat)* Stand the men down, captain. The grove is yours.
> **THESSA** *(from the grove center, without looking up)*: I saw you, Vael. I will remember what you did.
> *[No battle. Party rides away. At camp that night, Thessa's tent is empty; her staff leans against the door of Vael's, unspoken.]*

#### Post-battle (Defend):

> **THESSA:** *(ashes on her hands, fire out)* The crown burned the grove in your name, or the crown tried and your spears stopped them. Either way, you have entered the story. You don't get to leave.
> **VAEL:** I don't want to leave.
> **THESSA:** *(finally looking at her, a different look than any before)* Then I will teach you what the grove teaches. Don't waste it.

*[Observation: `moral_choice` = 'grove_defend' / 'grove_abandon'. Flag: `thessa_state`.]*

### BATTLE 8 — *"The Old Binding"*

**Location:** The inner vault of the Dark Rites (if Marrow alive) OR a forgotten Thorne family chapel under Ashreach (if Marrow was left to die). Either way: the place where the glyph that killed Torren is finally read aloud.

**Teaches:** all three schools in play; the reveal of Vellum's method on-screen.

**This is the hinge battle.** Every thread knotted in this scene. No branching.

#### Pre-battle (11 lines)

*(Dark Rites version — Marrow alive.)*

> **[The vault. A long stone table. On it: Marrow has laid out Torren's ledger pages — the red-brown ones from the opening cutscene — and is arranging them into a pattern only he can see.]**
>
> **MARROW:** Your brother was very brave.
> **VAEL:** *(not yet)*

## 5. THE ANTAGONIST — *Esra Vellum, "The Vellum"*

**The owned draft. Reasoning visible. Architect redirects at will.**

### The name

**Esra** — not the name she was born with. She took it when she became what she is. In the old tongue of the kingdoms, *Esra* meant a scribe who wrote on another's behalf — a court function, unremarkable. It was the title she held at twenty-two, before she learned that a thing written in the right hand on the right material would keep what it was bound to from dying, whether the bound one wanted to keep living or not.

**Vellum** — the material. Stretched skin, cured, writable. It is also what she calls her students: pages. It is also what she calls her victims: also pages. She does not distinguish. The register of the name is clinical — she is not cruel, she is technical, and that is worse.

### The silhouette

Not a body. Not anymore. Art direction:

- **At distance (Hollowcourt):** a pale smear of light, vaguely human-shaped, flickering in and out of focus. Suggest a figure in a scribe's long coat with sleeves rolled to the elbow. No face resolves. The viewer keeps almost-seeing and failing.
- **Close (the Crypt):** clearer. A woman of indeterminate age — could be fifty, could be two hundred. Hair short, once-severe, now thinning. The skin of her forearms is covered, elbow to wrist, in her own writing, layered and layered and layered, so deeply scripted it no longer reads as skin. Eyes are careful. The voice is a contralto, even. She does not raise it, ever.
- **When she speaks at Hollowcourt:** her voice is one-to-one, heard only in the ear of whoever she addresses. The party does not share her lines. This is an *audio* mechanic with a *narrative* function — isolation, personal address, guilt.

### The method — Vellum-script (two glyphs)

She writes bindings on human skin using a blend of bone-dust and her own saliva (a residue of her own attempt to bind herself, which cost her a body). Her script uses two canonical shapes. The difference between them is the difference between consent and coercion, and it is load-bearing.

**The Base Glyph — "the crossing."** A circle with one line drawn through it. This is her tether mark. It is the writing she lays on bodies that did not ask to be written on. It is the mark she put on Torren as a two-year-old at a royal visit and maintained, across the skin of his growing body, for thirty years. Variations nest inside and around the base glyph when a particular binding is tuned.

**The Student Mark — "the compact."** Three interlocking rings. This is the signature a student cuts into their own inner forearm when they swear themselves into her craft at seventeen. It is voluntary. It is not a tether. It declares the bearer as one of hers. It is worn visibly — sleeves rolled up, in her presence — because the craft she teaches is *read*, and a student who hides the mark has not accepted the craft.

On Vellum's own forearms, elbow to wrist, the layered writing is both glyphs overwritten countless times. She began with the student mark at twenty-two. She then tried to become fluent enough to un-write her own binding when the cost of what she had done began to come due. She could not. She overwrote the student mark with more and more dense script, trying to find a combination that would release her. The layering is a visible record of an escape attempt that failed. She laid the tether on Torren because she needed a living body to hold her written-in-the-world while she kept trying to solve her own equation. She never did.

**Player-recognizable canon for this script:**

- The red-brown on Torren's torn ledger pages (Opening Cutscene) — **base glyph.** He was scraping her marks off his own skin with a cup edge and hiding the script on paper.
- The **triple-loop tattoo on Marrow's inner forearm** — **student mark.** Chosen at seventeen. The sleeve-rolled visible declaration.
- The sleeve-back reveal on the dead lieutenant at Battle 5 — **student mark.** The viewer's first look at the shape.
- The seal at the bottom of the Grove-siege execution order (B7) — **base glyph.** Drawn by the Seneschal, but with her writing under the hand.
- The pattern on Hollowcourt's great-hall floor (B9) — **base glyph** cut into stone at scale.
- The single, cracked circle in the Thorne Crypt (B11) — **base glyph.** The originating door.

When a player re-watches the opening cutscene after B8, the red-brown smears on the torn pages resolve into *readable base-glyph writing they now recognize*. By B11, players can distinguish both glyphs on sight. The distinction matters: the base glyph is the thing done *to* someone. The student mark is the thing declared *by* someone. A character wearing the student mark has owned a choice. A character wearing the base glyph is being written.

### The three writings

Vellum-script is one literacy. The world has three.

1. **Vellum-script** — written on skin, bone, paper, stone. Writes on material that has stopped growing, or is about to. Fixes the thing written. Outlasts the writer. The only one weaponized into a tether rite, because only Vellum built that rite. Readable by Vellum, Marrow, Caelen (partial — technical not fluent), and by Vael after B8.

2. **Grove-script** — written as **knots** in living material: bark, sinew, hair, cloth, the weave of a still-growing vine. The knot tightens or loosens with the host's growth. Grove-script cannot fix the way Vellum-script fixes — a knot in living wood is alive, and alive means negotiable. The druids have read and written this for longer than there has been a kingdom. They never built a tether rite from it; the knot-work they do is closer to prayer than to binding. Thessa reads it natively. Her pendant (the nested-geometric medallion at her throat) is a knot resolved into metal — a grove signature she wears as a name. Her half-healed forearm scar is what happens when someone tries to tie a living knot into a human body and the human cuts it off in time (see §7 bond track).

3. **Old-One writing** — not laid onto a surface. Writes *itself* into whatever surface the language lives on. The ambient "un-writing" weather in B11 Phase 2 is not an attack; it is the language of the Old Ones arriving into a space where other writings already exist and overwriting them. Vellum cannot read it. The druids feel it but cannot write it. It is older than literacy. It does not distinguish page from writer.

This tri-layer is the reason B11 is solvable at all: three writings together can close a door one alone cannot. It is also the reason Vellum failed. She was fluent in one system and assumed it was the only system. Her pride is the pride of a scholar who stopped reading outside her field.

### The motive — and the reasoning for it

Prior Claude proposed *tether-work*: Vellum needs a living anchor to stay in our world; Torren was the tether; his death severed her line; Act 3 is her trying to re-tether through Vael. I tested that framing against MAGIC.md.

**MAGIC.md does not define "binding" as tether-work in its combat spells.** Its "Marrow's Binding" rite is corpse-raising, not live-tethering. But MAGIC.md §2 is explicit that the player's spell list is the Chapter 1 MVP. Nothing in MAGIC.md says Vellum's dark rites are confined to the player-accessible spells — in fact, §2 gives *the inner vault holds the knowledge that killed Vael's brother*, signaling knowledge beyond the Ch1 list.

So the tether-framing **does not contradict MAGIC.md.** It *extends* it — Vellum's method is a rite outside the player's available spell tree, narratively implied, mechanically unplayable. Good design.

I adopted it, and pushed it one step further. The additions I am owning:

1. **The tether requires continuity across generations.** Every Thorne heir since Vellum went under has worn the signet ring. Each wearer, on inheritance, has been marked without consent (she visited court decades ago; she had decades to mark children). The ring physically *shapes to the bound bearer* over years. Torren wore it thirty years; he resisted and scraped; he died in resistance. The ring was made loose again by his death — and is shaping itself to Vael across Act 1–2.
2. **Torren was not the first tether. He was the strongest.** Ledger pages from B8 imply the line goes back generations.
3. **She is the lesser door.** Behind her: the Old Ones (B11). She has been holding them back, not intentionally, as a side-effect of holding herself open. If she dies without the crypt being sealed, the door she was holding closed for twenty-eight years is unlocked. She is not the final problem. She is the problem you have to solve first because she is the one you can reach.
4. **Marrow's triple-loop forearm tattoo is her student mark.** He took it willingly at seventeen when he was her apprentice — a three-ring compact declared voluntarily, worn visibly. He hates her not because he regrets the marking but because **Torren did not get the choice**. Marrow's mark is a signature. Torren's was a leash. That asymmetry is the guilt he carries. That is why he volunteers to re-bind Torren's body at B11 — not from malice, but because he can close her line only by offering what he was spared.

### What she sounds like

Never gives a speech. Her longest monologue in the game is six lines (B11). Her most devastating lines are single sentences. See §8 for the voice-barb register — her lines follow the same rule the heroes' do.

Three sample lines she speaks (elsewhere in this doc, in-situ):

> *"Your brother had a harder voice than yours."* (B9, to Vael)
> *"You were my favorite. You still are."* (B9, to Marrow)
> *"The writing doesn't care whose finger it is on."* (B11, to Vael)

She does not gloat. She does not explain herself. She assumes her craft is visible and sees no reason to narrate it. That is what makes her dangerous — and what makes her unforgettable.

## 7. BOND-TRACK DIALOGUE TRIGGERS

Bonds accumulate through shared battles + camp interactions. Four bond levels: Acquainted / Trusted / Sworn / Soul. Each level unlocks one paired ability (mechanical, see MAGIC.md §4 hybrid-pair logic) and one camp dialogue scene.

### Vael + Thessa

A grove-knot, tied into her living body by someone who had learned enough grove-script to try the rite but not enough to understand what a knot does to a person who is still growing. She cut it off with a bone knife before it rooted. She names the person who tied it. They do not discuss it again.

They do it under the heartwood — Thessa teaches Vael a single grove-knot, tied into a length of green willow, the only grove-script Vael will ever know how to write. Enough for one locking. Enough for B11's substitution path if Thessa falls before the seal.

### Halv + Marrow

### Brin + Thessa

- **Paired** (B11 post-success): They fight back-to-back in the Thorne Crypt and one of them says nothing to the other for the entire fight and it is the closest either has ever been to anyone.

### Caelen + Marrow

- **Candid** (B8 vault scene): Caelen tells Marrow: *"If you choose wrongly in Act 3, I will be the one who stops you. I am sorry in advance."* Marrow says: *"I expected that. Thank you."* Later the same scene, quieter: Marrow points at a base-glyph cluster on Torren's ledger and reads it. Caelen watches. *"I studied her writing for a year,"* Caelen says. *"I never learned to read it."* Marrow: *"You weren't supposed to."*

## 8. VOICE-BARB STYLE GUIDE

**For scaling to 500+ combat lines across six heroes × campaign content without drift.**

### The four rules

1. **One sentence per barb. Maximum.** If it needs two, it is a cutscene line, not a barb.
2. **Action-register only.** No reflection mid-combat. *"Moving."* is a barb. *"I hope this works."* is not.
3. **Verb-forward or noun-forward.** Passive construction is out. *"He fell."* is fine. *"He was felled."* is cut.
4. **One named register per hero.** See table below. If a line drifts from the register, it is rewritten or cut.

### Per-hero register table (binding)

| Hero | Register | Keyword | Forbidden | Sample ✓ | Sample ✗ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Vael** | command, terse, never first-person-plural | *Hold. Forward. Mine.* | "we," exclamations | *"For the seat."* | *"We've got this!"* |
| **Halv** | professional infantry, dry, old | *Steady. Aye. Not past me.* | anything bookish | *"Not past me."* | *"Their line is fraying."* |
| **Brin** | scout, underreported, observational | *Moving. Got him. Seen.* | any emotional statement | *"Moving."* | *"I'm scared but I'll do it."* |
| **Caelen** | clinical, weather-report, monosyllabic under stress | *Down. Slow. Cover.* | metaphor, color | *"Down."* | *"Fire consumes all in its path."* |
| **Marrow** | formal, archaic, transactional, ironic under breath | *Serve me. Your name is written. Thank you.* | earnest vulnerability | *"Thank you."* *(ironic)* | *"I'm glad I could help."* |
| **Thessa** | imperative to nature, soft consonants, short | *Breathe. Go. Root.* | martial verbs | *"Breathe."* | *"Strike now!"* |
| **Vellum** *(antagonist)* | contralto, even, addresses by title or fact | *"Your brother had a harder voice."* *"My favorite."* | any threat | *"My favorite."* | *"You will all die!"* |

### Event-coverage matrix

Each hero needs, per campaign, at minimum:

- 6 attack barbs (one per primary weapon/spell — loaded at battle start)
- 4 critical-hit barbs
- 4 dodge/miss barbs
- 4 ally-fallen reactions (one per other party member)
- 3 self-wounded lines (HP 50%, 25%, 10%)
- 3 victory lines (rout, clean win, costly win)
- 3 defeat lines
- 6 biome-specific barbs (grove, tower, crypt, ridge, keep, court)
- 10 bond-state flavor barbs (gated by bond levels)
- 15 spell-specific barbs (casters — one per spell)
- 10 enemy-specific barbs (seeing a wraith for the first time, seeing Vellum-marked, seeing an Old-One weather, etc.)

Total per hero: ~60–80 for Ch1. 6 heroes × ~80 = ~480 core barbs. Vellum has ~20 (heard-in-ear lines, B9 + B11). Total: **~500.**

### The cut test

Before any barb is committed, the writer asks three questions:

1. **Would this hero say this under fire with no time?** If no, cut.
2. **Is the barb's verb the subject of the action?** If the verb is mood ("I'm scared"), cut.
3. **Could any other hero say this line?** If yes, rewrite until only this hero can.

Any line failing any test is cut. Not rewritten, cut. Scarcity is the register.

### Delegation prompt template for external-AI batch drafting

> *You are writing combat voice-barbs for a Final-Fantasy-Tactics-style game named Oath and Bone. You will be given a hero profile (HEROES.md entry), the register table (§8 of STORY.md), and a batch of events. You will produce one sentence per event, adhering to the hero's register keyword and forbidden list. You will not produce more than what is asked. You will not write speeches. You will not write any line that could be said by more than one hero. If you cannot produce a line that satisfies all rules, return the event ID with the string `BLANK` and we will not fill it.*

The `BLANK` return is load-bearing. It prevents drift-filler.

### The ring asset track — separate pipeline

The signet ring is the single most load-bearing visual object in the game (looseness = tether progress; §1, §4 B9, §6 Endings). It is not a portrait. It is a UI element that changes across four committed states:

- **State A (Act 1):** visibly loose. Rotates freely on gloved finger.
- **State B (mid-Act 2, post-B5):** still loose, less so. Stops rotating.
- **State C (post-B9):** fits. Ring no longer moves. UI portrait of Vael from this point forward shows the ring seated.
- **State D (ending-specific):**
  - *Endures*: fits, with a faint white scar ringing the finger beneath. The binding was burned off; the ring stays as inheritance.
  - *Falls*: fits perfectly. No scar. The binding took.
  - *Widens*: re-shaped back to Torren's size, loose again — a deliberate choice.

These four states are their own delivery — macro shot, consistent framing, consistent hand, consistent lighting, swappable. A UI overlay lets the game render the current state on the Vael card, on cutscene hand-shots, and in the ending montage. This pipeline is separate from portraits and should be commissioned as its own briefs.
