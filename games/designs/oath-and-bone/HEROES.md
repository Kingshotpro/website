# Oath and Bone — Heroes

*Six playable heroes at Chapter 1 MVP launch. Four recruited during Acts 2–3 (sketched). All original — no canonical Kingshot heroes. Unofficial. Not affiliated with Century Games.*

---

## Design principles

1. Each hero carries story weight equal to their mechanical role.
2. Permadeath is real. Losses cost mechanically AND narratively.
3. No chosen-one framing. Vael is protagonist by inheritance, not prophecy.
4. Voice barbs, not speeches. One sentence max in combat.
5. Moral geometry — characters don't align on good/evil, but on what they will and won't do.

---

## 1. Vael Thorne — Protagonist

**Job:** Knight (base) → Paladin (Lv 15) / Death Knight hybrid / Battlemage hybrid

**Stats:** HP 120, Def 8, Atk 12, Move 3
**Signatures:** Vanguard's Oath (adjacent allies +4 Def while Vael stands; 1 Resolve/turn); Braced Charge (3-hex charge + 18 dmg, CD 2)
**Troop tag:** infantry (base), cavalry (promoted). No magic primary; can learn secondary magic via hybrid.

**Story:** 26-year-old. Inherited the vassal seat of Ashreach eight weeks after her brother Torren died at a crossing that shouldn't have been contested. Signet ring one size too large. She grieves sideways — never discusses Torren unless prompted. The ledger's missing pages lead her into a war she wasn't told existed.

Arc: recognition that the war has a shape beneath its surface. By Act 2 she's seen magic. By Act 3 she decides how deep she'll step.

**Voice barbs:**
- Braced Charge: *"For the seat."*
- Ally fallen: *"Get up. We're not done."*
- Counter-crit: *"That's the opening."*

**Portrait:** half-armor, muted kingdom palette, gold at the collar. Three-quarter, controlled expression, looking off-frame.

**Permadeath:** game-over if Vael dies. Player warned explicitly.

## 2. Sergeant Halv — Vael's brother's second

**Job:** Warrior → Captain (Lv 15) / Sentinel (Lv 20) / Battlemage if pushed

**Stats:** HP 160, Def 10, Atk 14, Move 3
**Signatures:** Hold the Line (taunt + adjacent enemies can't move past threatened tiles); Cleaving Stroke (primary + adjacent-hex in same direction, CD 3)
**Troop tag:** infantry. No magic. Distrusts it.

**Story:** 44. Served under Torren twelve years. Trained Vael at ten. Was at the crossing when Torren died and hasn't slept a full night since. Deeply suspicious of magic — cordial with Caelen, troubled by Marrow. If the player routes too much magic too fast, Halv's loyalty track drops. He won't leave. His voice barbs go flat.

Arc: whether loyalty to the dead lord means protecting the living one from the world, or trusting her to know what the world requires.

**Voice barbs:**
- Hold the Line: *"Not past me."*
- Magic caster working: *"If you say so, my lord."* (later, flatter: *"If you say so."*)
- Winning: *"Good work."*

**Portrait:** weathered, grey-streaked beard, iron-and-grey kit, small sergeant's pin. Honest fatigue.

**Permadeath:** losable. Narrative loss — Vael's form of address at camp changes for remainder of chapter.

## 3. Brin Fletcher — Orphan scout

**Job:** Ranger → Sharpshooter (Lv 15) / Skirmisher (Lv 20) / Warden hybrid

**Stats:** HP 95, Def 5, Atk 13 (bow), Move 4
**Signatures:** Loose and Fade (attack + 2-hex reposition without opportunity attacks, CD 2); Called Shot (skip 1 turn, +60% damage, guaranteed hit)
**Troop tag:** archer. No magic. Not afraid of it either.

**Story:** 19. Orphaned at 8 in a borderland raid; Torren's household fostered her. She was the scout whose report drew Torren to the fatal crossing. She blames herself and will never say so. Knows the hollows and the grove. She's the party's guide to the hidden world.

Arc: what loyalty looks like when you've been the reason someone died. She'll do anything for Vael. The question is whether Vael asks her to do what Brin knows is wrong.

**Voice barbs:**
- Loose and Fade: *"Moving."*
- Called Shot hit: *"Got him."*
- Grove battle: *"I knew this place would matter."*

**Portrait:** young, hard-eyed, leather armor, scar through the left brow, bowstring calluses.

**Permadeath:** losable. Combat penalty on hollow/grove battles if she dies (lost terrain knowledge).

## 4. Caelen the Quiet — Wizard

**Job:** Wizard → Elementalist (Lv 15) / Arcanist (Lv 20) / Battlemage hybrid

**Stats:** HP 70, Def 4, Atk 6, Move 3. Mana 40, Regen 3 (+3 on sanctum/ruin).
**Starting spells:** Firebolt, Frost Shard, Spark, Shield
**Troop tag:** wizard (magic primary, Wizardry school)

**Story:** Looks mid-thirties, is older. Last heir to a tower the kingdoms believed decommissioned a generation ago. He did not go dark — he chose silence. He joins the party during Act 1 Battle 5 because something has come for the tower that it can't weather alone. He knows it's connected to what killed Torren but won't say how.

Speaks rarely. When he speaks, it matters. Does not banter or reassure. Gives weather reports: *"That ritual is not finished."*

Arc: whether he should have come down from the tower twenty years ago. The ending reveals what he chose instead.

**Voice barbs:**
- Fireball: *"Down."*
- Frost Shard: *"Slow."*
- Shield: *"Cover me."*
- Ally falling: *"...I should have cast sooner."*

**Portrait:** gaunt, severe, deep undereye shadows. Once-fine robes worn thin. Focus-crystal on a chain.

**Permadeath:** losable. Primary damage dealer — some Act 2–3 battles become borderline unwinnable. Strongly warned.

## 5. Marrow — Necromancer

**Job:** Necromancer → Binder (Lv 15) / Ruinmancer (Lv 20) / Death Knight hybrid

**Stats:** HP 80, Def 5, Atk 7, Move 3. Souls cap 30 (+5/enemy killed by any ally).
**Starting spells:** Raise Skeleton, Curse of Weakness, Life Drain, Bone Shield
**Troop tag:** necromancer (magic primary, Necromancy school)

**Story:** 31. Imprisoned in a rival kingdom's dungeon when the party finds him, marked for execution. Guilty of what he's accused of. Won't pretend otherwise. Former student of the necromancer who laid the binding that killed Torren. Joins because killing his old teacher is the only thing he wants more than the party wants to stop her. Alliance is transactional and both parties know it.

Does not apologize. Does not claim redemption. Thessa watches him like she's deciding whether to kill him in his sleep. Halv won't drink with him. Caelen alone speaks to him without reservation.

Arc: whether transactional alliance becomes something else, or whether using a necromancer means accepting the cost of having used one.

**Voice barbs:**
- Raise Skeleton: *"Serve me."*
- Curse: *"Your name is written."*
- Life Drain: *"Thank you."* (ironic)
- To Thessa: *"Your grove will still stand when this is done."*

**Portrait:** severe, composed, not theatrically sinister — the face of a man who decided something unforgivable and lives with it on purpose. Triple-loop student-mark tattoo on the inner forearm — three interlocking rings, worn visibly — his sworn declaration into Vellum's craft at seventeen. See STORY.md §5 *The method* for glyph canon.

**Permadeath:** losable. Act 3 opens alternate "Wizard Path" via Caelen if Marrow dies — harder but survivable.

## 6. Thessa of the Hollow — Druid

**Job:** Druid → Shepherd (Lv 15) / Greenwarden (Lv 20) / Warden hybrid

**Stats:** HP 95, Def 6, Atk 8 (staff), Move 3. Verdance cap 35 (+5/turn adjacent to natural terrain).
**Starting spells:** Heal, Regrowth, Summon Wolf, Gale
**Troop tag:** druid (magic primary, Druidry school)

**Story:** 27. Survivor of the siege that drove her druid circle from the grove. Stayed when the others fled. Maintains the grove alone. Joins in Act 1 B3 or Act 2 B7 depending on branching. Joins with expectations, not gratitude.

Watches Vael. Decides whether the kingdom deserves the grove's effort. Does not pretend to be neutral. If Vael protects the grove and respects the druid dead, Thessa becomes the party's moral compass. If Vael leans into necromancy too far, Thessa becomes the warning.

Arc: what a survivor owes the world she survived. Twenty years ago she chose not to leave. The game asks her to choose again.

**Voice barbs:**
- Heal: *"Breathe."*
- Summon Wolf: *"Go."*
- Marrow casting: *"I'm watching."*
- Grove battle: *"You don't know what this place was."*

**Portrait:** natural, weather-lined, freckled, braided hair, nested-geometric grove-knot pendant at the throat (a grove-script signature cast in metal; see STORY.md §5 *The three writings*). Half-healed knot-scar on one forearm — unexplained at first look; revealed on bond track (STORY.md §7 Vael+Thessa Trusted).

**Permadeath:** losable. Loss of primary healer — Act 2–3 difficulty rises sharply. Grove-related story beats collapse.

## Chapter 2–3 recruits (sketched)

Full bios deferred to next-Claude expansion. Design seeds:

**7. Kavess** — Rogue → Spellblade hybrid. Disgraced southern-court scout. Flexible loyalties; surprising depth. Act 2 recruit.

**8. Talia Brenwyn** — Bard → Loremaster/Chronicler. Unique resource: **Verses** (earned by observing enemy actions). Party-buff specialist. Carries Act 3 cutscene weight.

## Party composition guidance (Chapter 1)

Tuned for parties of 5–6 from the Ch1 six. Recommended loadouts:

- **"The Line"** — Vael + Halv + Brin + Caelen + Thessa — balanced default.
- **"The Cabal"** — Vael + Brin + Caelen + Marrow + Thessa — magic-heavy, rewards skill.
- **"The Seat"** — Vael + Halv + 2 others — mundane-heavy. Halv sharpens if Marrow excluded.

No composition punished. Magic-heavy surfaces more grove/tower content; martial-heavy shifts advisor barbs. Player preference observed → `hero_composition`.
